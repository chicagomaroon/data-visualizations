What crimes are not included on this map?
The only crimes included on the map are violent, property, and drug crimes, which are about half of the about 70,000 crimes reported around the University since 2001. The vast majority of crimes not included on this map fall into one of the following categories: trespassing, prostitution, weapons violations, criminal damage, deceptive practices, or peace violations.

What is defined as “around the University?”
47th Street on the north to 63rd Street on the south and Cottage Grove on the west to the Lake on the east. This definition includes the southern part of the Kenwood neighborhood, the entirety of Hyde Park, and north Woodlawn.

What is the difference between robbery, burglary, and theft?
According to the FBI's Uniform Crime Reporting Handbook, robbery is the taking or attempting to take anything of value from someone by force or threat of force or violence and/or by putting the victim in fear. Burglary is defined as the unlawful entry of a structure to commit a felony or a theft. Theft is the unlawful taking of property from the possession of another.

What is the difference between assault and battery?
According to Illinois law, assault is conduct which places another in reasonable apprehension of receiving a battery. Battery is defined as conduct causing bodily harm to another person.

Does this data also include crimes to which the University of Chicago Police Department responded?
Yes, it includes all crimes reported to have occurred within the City, regardless of the responding agency.

Where is this data from?
This data comes directly from the City of Chicago’s data portal, using the dataset “Crime - 2001 to present,” which includes all crimes reported to have occurred within the City from January 1, 2001 to the present, minus the past seven days.
