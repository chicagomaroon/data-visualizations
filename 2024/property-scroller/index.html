When the University of Chicago was founded in 1890, it was envisioned as six quadrangles, each surrounded by buildings, bordering one larger quadrangle built on 22 acres of land.
Today, the University has owned or managed over 200 properties and buildings in Hyde Park and the surrounding neighborhoods, spanning from 65th Street in the south, 38th Street in the north, Lake Michigan in the east, and I-90 to the west.
How did the University come to own all this property and what role has it played in shaping Hyde Park and the surrounding neighborhoods?
The University of Chicago as Urban Planner
The story of how the University of Chicago played an active role in shaping the Hyde Park neighborhood.
Written by Celeste Alcalay, Austin Steinhart, and Nikhil Patel
Design and Development by Austin Steinhart and Nikhil Patel

How did the University come to own its current property? How have the boundaries of campus changed over time? As conflicting priorities, land use disputes, and racial issues have driven its physical expansion and the evolution of a historically fraught “town and gown” relationship, UChicago has had an active role in shaping Hyde Park and the surrounding communities.

This interactive map documents UChicago’s property acquisition from its 1890 charter to the present. The accompanying narration is not an exhaustive history but traces the contours of shifting university objectives.

Methodology

The data for this project was sourced from the University of Chicago’s property records, which are available online. The property records were then geocoded and visualized using the Mapbox GL JS library. The timeline was created using the Waypoints library. The project was built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
