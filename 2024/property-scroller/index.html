When the University of Chicago was founded in 1890, there were 14 original buildings built on 22 acres of land.
Today, UChicago owns or manages over 200 properties and buildings from 65th street in the south, 38st in the north, Lake Michigan in east, and I-90 to the west.
How did UChicago come to own all this property and what role has the university played in shaping the neighborhood of Hyde Park?

How did the University come to own its current property? UChicago has had an active role in shaping the built environment of Hyde Park and other surrounding communities, as conflicting priorities, land use disputes, and racial issues have driven its physical expansion and influenced the evolution of a historically fraught “town and gown” relationship.
